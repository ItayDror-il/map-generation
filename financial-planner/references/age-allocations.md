# Age-Based Allocation Guidelines

## 18-24: Foundation Building

**Profile:** Entry-level jobs, student loans, building credit
**Primary goals:** Build habits, starter emergency fund, avoid lifestyle creep

| Category | % of Income | Notes |
|----------|-------------|-------|
| Needs (rent, food, transport) | 50-60% | May be higher in HCOL areas |
| Emergency fund | 10% | Until $1,000-$2,000 saved |
| Debt minimum payments | Required | Student loans, car |
| Retirement (if employer match) | Up to match | Free money |
| Savings/goals | 5-10% | After emergency fund |
| Wants | 20-30% | Keep lifestyle lean |

**Key actions:**
- Open Roth IRA (tax-free growth for decades)
- Build credit responsibly
- Avoid car loans if possible
- Start tracking spending

---

## 25-34: Acceleration Phase

**Profile:** Career growth, higher income, possibly starting family
**Primary goals:** Aggressive debt payoff, max retirement contributions, save for house

| Category | % of Income | Notes |
|----------|-------------|-------|
| Needs | 45-50% | Should decrease as income rises |
| Debt payoff (beyond minimums) | 10-20% | Prioritize high-interest |
| Retirement | 15-20% | Max 401k if possible ($23,000/yr) |
| Emergency fund | 5-10% | Build to 3-4 months |
| House down payment | 10-15% | If homeownership goal |
| Wants | 15-20% | Reasonable lifestyle |

**Key actions:**
- Eliminate all non-mortgage debt
- Max employer 401k match minimum
- Build 3-month emergency fund
- Consider house purchase timing
- Start taxable brokerage if maxing retirement

---

## 35-44: Peak Earning Years

**Profile:** Established career, family expenses, college planning
**Primary goals:** Balance current needs with future security

| Category | % of Income | Notes |
|----------|-------------|-------|
| Needs | 40-50% | Mortgage, childcare, insurance |
| Retirement | 20-25% | Catch up if behind |
| College savings (529) | 5-10% | If children |
| Emergency fund maintenance | 2-5% | Keep 4-5 months |
| Additional investments | 10-15% | Taxable brokerage |
| Wants | 15-20% | Family activities, travel |

**Key actions:**
- Review life insurance needs
- Estate planning (will, beneficiaries)
- Diversify investment accounts (pre-tax, Roth, taxable)
- Consider umbrella insurance
- Avoid lifestyle inflation with raises

---

## 45-54: Catch-Up Phase

**Profile:** Kids leaving home, peak income, retirement visible
**Primary goals:** Maximize retirement savings, reduce risk

| Category | % of Income | Notes |
|----------|-------------|-------|
| Needs | 35-45% | May decrease as kids leave |
| Retirement | 25-30% | Catch-up contributions ($7,500 extra) |
| Healthcare savings (HSA) | Max | Triple tax advantage |
| Emergency fund | Maintain | 5-6 months |
| Debt elimination | Priority | Pay off mortgage if possible |
| Wants | 15-20% | Travel, hobbies |

**Key actions:**
- Use catch-up contributions (401k: +$7,500, IRA: +$1,000)
- Shift to more conservative allocation (bonds)
- Pay off mortgage before retirement
- Model retirement income needs
- Consider long-term care insurance

---

## 55-64: Pre-Retirement

**Profile:** Approaching retirement, wealth preservation focus
**Primary goals:** Protect assets, plan distribution strategy

| Category | % of Income | Notes |
|----------|-------------|-------|
| Needs | 35-40% | Should be lowest |
| Retirement | 15-20% | Continue maxing |
| Healthcare reserves | 10% | Medicare gap planning |
| Cash/bonds buffer | Build | 2-3 years expenses |
| Wants | 20-25% | Enjoy while healthy |

**Key actions:**
- Build 2-3 year cash buffer for sequence risk
- Plan Social Security claiming strategy
- Understand Medicare enrollment windows
- Consider Roth conversions for tax diversification
- Test retirement budget with "practice retirement"

---

## 65+: Distribution Phase

**Profile:** Retired or semi-retired, drawing down assets
**Primary goals:** Sustainable withdrawals, legacy planning

| Category | % of Portfolio Withdrawal | Notes |
|----------|---------------------------|-------|
| Safe withdrawal rate | 3.5-4% | Adjust for market conditions |
| Required Minimum Distributions | As required | Start at 73 |
| Healthcare | Plan for $300k+ lifetime | Major expense |
| Legacy/giving | As desired | Charitable strategies |

**Key actions:**
- Follow RMD rules carefully (penalties are severe)
- Optimize withdrawal sequence (taxable → tax-deferred → Roth)
- Review estate plan annually
- Consider qualified charitable distributions
- Maintain some growth allocation (longevity risk)
