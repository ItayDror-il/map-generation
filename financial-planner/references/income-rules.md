# Income-Based Financial Rules

## The 50/30/20 Framework (Baseline)

Standard allocation for W-2 employees:
- **50% Needs**: Rent/mortgage, utilities, groceries, insurance, minimum debt payments
- **30% Wants**: Entertainment, dining, hobbies, subscriptions
- **20% Savings/Debt**: Emergency fund, retirement, extra debt payments

**Adjust based on income level and cost of living.**

---

## Income Tier: Under $50,000

**Challenges:** Tight margins, limited flexibility, benefits gaps
**Strategy:** Maximize efficiency, build foundation slowly

| Category | Target % | Monthly ($4,000 gross) |
|----------|----------|------------------------|
| Needs | 55-60% | $2,200-$2,400 |
| Savings | 10-15% | $400-$600 |
| Debt payoff | 5-10% | $200-$400 |
| Wants | 20-25% | $800-$1,000 |

**Emergency fund target:** 3 months expenses (~$6,600-$7,200)

**Priority actions:**
1. Build $1,000 starter emergency fund first
2. Get any employer 401k match (free money)
3. Pay minimums on all debt
4. Slowly build to 3 months emergency fund
5. Then attack highest interest debt

**Tax considerations:**
- Likely in 12% bracket
- Prioritize Roth contributions (pay tax now while low)
- Check eligibility for Saver's Credit
- EITC if applicable

## Income Tier: $50,000 - $100,000

**Challenges:** Lifestyle inflation temptation, benefit optimization
**Strategy:** Aggressive debt elimination, establish strong savings habits

| Category | Target % | Monthly ($6,250 gross) |
|----------|----------|------------------------|
| Needs | 45-50% | $2,800-$3,125 |
| Savings/Investing | 20% | $1,250 |
| Debt payoff | 10-15% | $625-$940 |
| Wants | 20-25% | $1,250-$1,560 |

**Emergency fund target:** 4 months expenses (~$11,200-$12,500)

**Priority actions:**
1. Max employer 401k match
2. Build 4-month emergency fund
3. Eliminate all high-interest debt (>7%)
4. Max Roth IRA ($7,000/year)
5. Increase 401k to 15% of income

**Tax considerations:**
- Likely in 22% bracket
- Traditional 401k reduces taxable income
- Consider Roth vs Traditional based on expected future income
- HSA if available (triple tax advantage)

---

## Income Tier: $100,000 - $250,000

**Challenges:** Tax efficiency, avoiding lifestyle creep, phase-outs
**Strategy:** Maximize tax-advantaged accounts, diversify account types

| Category | Target % | Monthly ($12,500 gross) |
|----------|----------|-------------------------|
| Needs | 35-40% | $4,375-$5,000 |
| Savings/Investing | 25-30% | $3,125-$3,750 |
| Debt payoff | 5-10% | $625-$1,250 |
| Wants | 25-30% | $3,125-$3,750 |

**Emergency fund target:** 5 months expenses (~$21,875-$25,000)

**Priority actions:**
1. Max 401k ($23,000)
2. Max HSA if eligible ($4,150 individual / $8,300 family)
3. Backdoor Roth IRA if over income limit
4. Build taxable brokerage account
5. Consider mega backdoor Roth if employer allows

**Tax considerations:**
- Likely in 24-32% bracket
- Roth IRA income limits may apply (backdoor strategy)
- Maximize pre-tax deductions
- Consider tax-loss harvesting in taxable accounts
- Watch for AMT if heavy state/local taxes

---

## Income Tier: $250,000+

**Challenges:** Complex tax planning, concentration risk, estate planning
**Strategy:** Comprehensive wealth building, tax optimization, protection

| Category | Target % | Monthly ($25,000 gross) |
|----------|----------|-------------------------|
| Needs | 25-35% | $6,250-$8,750 |
| Savings/Investing | 35-45% | $8,750-$11,250 |
| Tax optimization | 5-10% | $1,250-$2,500 |
| Wants | 20-25% | $5,000-$6,250 |

**Emergency fund target:** 6 months expenses (~$37,500-$52,500)

**Priority actions:**
1. Max all tax-advantaged accounts
2. Backdoor Roth + Mega backdoor if available
3. Taxable investing with tax efficiency focus
4. Consider deferred compensation if offered
5. Estate planning (trusts, gifting strategies)
6. Umbrella insurance ($1M+ coverage)

**Tax considerations:**
- Likely in 32-37% bracket
- Net Investment Income Tax (3.8%) applies
- Qualified dividends and long-term gains preferred
- Consider charitable giving strategies (DAF)
- State tax planning if in high-tax state
- Estimated tax payments required

## Self-Employment Tax Reserves

Self-employed individuals must set aside additional funds for:
- Self-employment tax: 15.3% (Social Security + Medicare)
- Federal income tax: Based on bracket
- State income tax: Varies by state
- Quarterly estimated payments: Required

**Recommended tax reserve by income:**

| Self-Employment Income | Reserve % | Notes |
|------------------------|-----------|-------|
| Under $50k | 20-25% | Lower bracket + SE tax |
| $50k-$100k | 25-30% | Middle bracket + SE tax |
| $100k-$250k | 30-35% | Higher bracket + SE tax |
| $250k+ | 35-40% | Top brackets + NIIT |

**Deduction opportunities:**
- SEP-IRA or Solo 401k (up to $66,000)
- Health insurance premiums (100% deductible)
- Home office deduction
- Business expenses
- QBI deduction (20% of qualified income)
